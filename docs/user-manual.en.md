# La Ruche User Manual

This guide is written for everyday users. You do not need technical knowledge to use La Ruche.

## What La Ruche is for

La Ruche is a collaborative tool designed to support everyone involved in the Cultural and Artistic Education Pathway (PECA) in creating and implementing high-quality cultural and artistic experiences. It is intended for all PECA stakeholders and encourages reflection, exchange, documentation, and evaluation throughout the entire process. Accessible to everyone, it invites users to try things out, experiment, and enrich educational and cultural practices, alone or in a team. You can move cards, add your own cards, save your work, and share a hive with other people.

## Before you start

- Open the application in your browser.
- If needed, change the interface language in the footer.
- You can open `GDPR / Privacy` and `Work in Progress` from the landing page or footer.
- Create an account or sign in.

## Create an account

1. Click `Create account`.
2. Fill in your username, email address, first name, last name, role, and password.
3. Click the confirmation button.
4. After registration, you are sent to your profile.

## Sign in

1. Click `Sign in`.
2. Enter your username or email address.
3. Enter your password.
4. Click the sign-in button.

If you forgot your password, use `Forgot password?`. If email delivery is configured, you will receive a reset link. If not, contact your project administrator.

## Your profile

Your profile is your main starting point.

From this page, you can:

- See your own hives
- See hives shared with you
- Open an existing hive
- Create a new hive
- Duplicate a hive
- Download an export of a hive
- Delete a hive
- Update your role or password

## Create a new hive

1. Go to your profile.
2. Click `Create a new Hive`.
3. Enter a title.
4. If your role allows it, you may also choose the hive type.
5. The editor opens.

## Working in the editor

The editor has two main areas:

- The card library
- The board

To build your hive:

1. Drag a card from the library to the board.
2. Move cards until the board matches your idea.
3. Use free cards if you want to write your own content.
4. Save regularly.

Useful things to know:

- A hive is not shared automatically. You decide when to invite people.
- If you try to leave with unsaved changes, the app warns you.
- Some users can create a DCO hive. In that case, the available cards are adapted automatically.

## Desktop vs Tablet

La Ruche adapts to your device. Whether you use a desktop computer (with mouse) or a tablet (with touchscreen), the interface and interactions change.

**On desktop:**

- You can use your keyboard to select multiple cards (Ctrl+Click, Ctrl+A)
- You can drag and drop easily with your mouse
- You have access to all keyboard shortcuts

**On tablet:**

- Interactions are touch-based (tap and drag)
- You can select cards in the library by tapping them
- Once cards are selected, options appear to:
  - Place them on the board
  - Put them back in the library
  - Move them together

## Select multiple cards

**On desktop:**

You can select multiple cards using your keyboard:

- **Ctrl+Click** (or **Cmd+Click** on Mac): Click on a card while holding Ctrl to add it or remove it from your selection. You can select multiple cards individually this way.
- **Ctrl+A** (or **Cmd+A** on Mac): Select all cards on the board at once.

**On tablet:**

You can select multiple cards from the library:

1. Tap on the cards you want to select in the library (a counter shows the number of selected cards)
2. Once your cards are selected, tap the button to place them on the board or perform another action
3. You can also select cards on the board by enabling the "Multi-select" toggle to move them together

## Add your own card

You can create free cards for your own notes or ideas.

1. In the library, choose the option for a free card.
2. Enter your text.
3. Choose a color.
4. Confirm.

The card is added to the board and can be moved like the other cards.

## Card notes

Each card can hold a note.

Use card notes when you want to add a short explanation directly to one card.

Important:

- Card notes are meant for saved hives.
- If you remove a card that contains a note, La Ruche asks for confirmation.

## Comments

Each hive also has a comments area.

Use comments when you want to discuss the hive with other people.

Depending on your access rights, you can:

- Read comments
- Add a comment
- Reply to a comment

## Save your hive

When you save, your work is stored in your account.

After saving, you can come back later and continue working from your profile.

## Share a hive

You can invite other users by email.

Possible access roles:

- `Administrator`: can manage collaboration on the hive
- `Collaborator (can edit)`: can modify the hive
- `Comment only`: can discuss but not edit the board
- `Read only`: can only view

To share a hive:

1. Open the hive.
2. Click the collaborators button.
3. Enter the person's email address.
4. Choose the access role.
5. Send the invitation.

## Invitations

If someone shares a hive with you:

1. Open `Inbox`.
2. Review the invitation.
3. Accept or decline.

Accepted hives appear in the shared section of your profile.

## Export and download

You can export a hive from the editor or from your profile.

Exports can include:

- The board image
- Hive comments
- Card notes

This is useful for sharing or archiving a finished board.

## Admin area

Admin users have an extra administration page.

From there, they can:

- Search users
- Search hives
- Edit some user details
- Rename hives
- Delete users or hives

## Simple tips

- Save before closing the page.
- Use clear hive titles so you can find them later.
- Check your inbox when you expect a shared hive.
- Use card notes for details on one card and hive comments for group discussion.
- Change the language from the footer if needed, and use the footer links for `GDPR / Privacy` and `Work in Progress`.

## If something goes wrong

- If you cannot sign in, check your email address, username, and password.
- If a page seems blocked, refresh the browser once.
- If you do not receive a password reset email, check your spam folder and confirm that email sending is enabled for your environment.
- If an invitation does not appear, open the inbox again after a few seconds.
